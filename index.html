Filipino Pronoun Meaning Explanation
Ako I / Me This is the standard or emphatic form of "I" or "me." It is used when the pronoun is the subject of the sentence or used on its own for emphasis (e.g., "Ako si Jerry" - I am Jerry).
Ko My / Me (Non-subject) This is the enclitic or non-subject form. It generally means "my" (possessive, e.g., "Bahay ko" - My house) or is used as the object of a verb (e.g., "Gusto ko" - I like/want).
First Person | Plural
  1. Tayo (Inclusive): Means "we/us" and includes the person you are talking to (the listener).
  2. Kami (Exclusive): Means "we/us" but excludes the person you are talking to (the listener).
